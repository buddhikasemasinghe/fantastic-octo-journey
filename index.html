The AI Landscape

Understanding where Large Language Models fit in.

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): Systems that simulate human intelligence.
  • Machine Learning (ML): A subset of AI where systems learn from data without explicit programming.
  • Deep Learning (DL): A subset of ML using neural networks with many layers.
  • Large Language Models (LLM): Deep neural networks specialized in understanding and generating human-like text.
  • Generative AI (GenAI): Involves deep neural networks to create new content (text, images, media).

What is a Large Language Model (LLM)?

At its core, an LLM is a type of AI algorithm that uses deep learning techniques and a massive amount of text data to understand, summarize, generate, and predict new content.

They excel at tasks like translation, summarization, question answering, and creative writing by learning intricate patterns and structures in human language.

Basic Concepts: Tokens

LLMs don't process text character by character or even word by word in the traditional sense. They break down text into smaller units called tokens.

  • A token can be a whole word, a sub-word (like "ing" or "un"), or even a single character or punctuation mark.
  • The process of converting text into tokens is called tokenization.

Basic Concepts: Embeddings

Once text is tokenized, each token is converted into a numerical representation called an embedding.

  • Embeddings are high-dimensional vectors that capture the semantic meaning and contextual relationships of words.
  • Words with similar meanings or used in similar contexts will have embeddings that are "closer" to each other in this multi-dimensional space.
